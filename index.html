Campus Life

school church

was established in 1978

St Francis Xavier Church was designed by architect Andrea Stombuco. The foundation stone for St Patricks's (as it was originally called) was laid on Sunday 15 August 1880 by Bishop of Brisbane James Quinn. The church was opened in Sunday 1 May 1881 by Bishop Quinn.

school library

was built in 1980

The college is part of the international network of Jesuit schools begun in Messina, Sicily in 1548. Originally an all-boys school, the College now offers co-education until Year 4, and an all-boys environment from then on. In 2011, the school had 2,085 students on roll, including 76 boarders.

school Auditoruim

was built in 1982

The stage has always been held in great honour at St. Xavier’s. For an educational institution an auditorium is not just a venue for school or college events, or merely a meeting place, it is a space where a corporation of students and scholars congregate – to celebrate, to debate, to discuss, and sometimes even to mourn collectively.

What's New

  • The New Science Learning Centre

    The newly opened Science Learning Centre will transforn science education at St Xaviers College. These twenty-first century educational facility is essential to preparing the next generation to tackle and solve current world problems as creative thinkers, innovators and pioneers.
    The Science Learning Centre significantly increases the number of laboratories at our school with specialised equipment for each scientific discipline, and ensuring our students are prepared for the world in which they live and work.

  • Upgrade to the School Auditorium

    St Xavier's School Auditorium has not been refurbished since it was established in 1985. The planning and consultation have been ongoing for the past 5 years, so we are excited for the upcoming completion of this highly anticipated structure.
